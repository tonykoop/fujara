# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single tall Slovak fujara overtone flute standing nearly vertical as a finished physical object, photographed in a clean neutral studio. The instrument is a very long, slender stave-built wooden cylinder — close to two meters tall — with a smooth turned hardwood surface showing warm North American hardwood grain and subtle vertical glue lines from the staves. Its signature recognizable feature is the thin secondary air tube running parallel to the main bore for roughly the upper third of the length, joined to the main body at a sunken nest where the side tube meets the labium; a side-blown mouthpiece sits at the very top of this parallel tube. The main bore shows an open foot end at the bottom and a small cut labium / sound-hole near the top of the long chamber, with only three modest finger holes spaced along the lower body. Hand-rubbed oiled finish, slightly satin sheen, a faint chamfer at the open foot. Realistic scale cues — the flute is taller than an adult, leaning gently against a plain seamless backdrop. Soft directional studio light from the upper left, gentle falloff, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the fujara's signature voicing zone: the junction where the thin parallel side air tube meets the main bore at the sunken nest, with the cut labium / sound-hole edge on the main body just below it. Show the precise beveled edge of the labium, the small rectangular sound-hole opening, the flue gap delivering the air jet, and the turned hardwood grain with faint stave glue lines around the nest. Raking low-angle light to reveal the edge geometry and surface texture, very shallow depth of field, high detail, no measurements, no callouts.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines, measurement callouts, or tuning numbers; no impossible mechanisms; no extra finger holes beyond three; the side air tube is an air-delivery branch, not a second bell — keep it thin and parallel; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
